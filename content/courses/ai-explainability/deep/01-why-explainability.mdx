## The Black Box Problem: Why AI Needs to Show Its Work

Only 20% of organizations have deployed AI into production workflows that touch real customers. The other 80% are stuck — not because models don't work, but because they can't **explain what the AI is doing** well enough for compliance, audits, or production trust.

<AnalogyBox emoji="🏥" title="Doctor's Diagnosis vs Lab Report">
Imagine a doctor who says "Take this medication" without explaining why. You'd want to know: What's the diagnosis? What evidence supports it? What are the risks? AI explainability answers the same questions about model predictions: What did the model decide? What evidence did it use? How confident is it? What could change the outcome?
</AnalogyBox>

<AnalogyBox emoji="📊" title="Data Lineage for AI">
In data governance at McKesson, you track data lineage: where did this data come from, what transformations were applied, who approved it? AI explainability is data lineage for predictions: what training data shaped this output, what features drove this decision, what intermediate reasoning steps occurred?
</AnalogyBox>

### The EU AI Act Connection

The EU AI Act (enforcement starting August 2026) requires explainability for high-risk AI systems — credit scoring, medical diagnosis, hiring decisions. Organizations deploying AI in the EU must demonstrate they can explain model outputs to regulators. This isn't optional.
