## What Is ACP?

<AnalogyBox emoji="📡" title="Enterprise Message Bus">If A2A is agents exchanging business cards and having conversations, ACP is like a corporate event-driven message bus -- agents publish events, subscribe to topics, and communicate asynchronously at enterprise scale.</AnalogyBox>

The <JargonTip term="ACP">Agent Communication Protocol (ACP)</JargonTip> by IBM focuses on **event-driven, async-first messaging** for enterprise <JargonTip term="agent">agent</JargonTip> ecosystems. While <JargonTip term="A2A">A2A</JargonTip> handles bilateral agent conversations, ACP handles <JargonTip term="multi-agent">multi-agent</JargonTip> event streams.

- **📡 Event-Driven** -- Agents publish events, others subscribe -- loose coupling at scale
- **⏰ Async-First** -- Non-blocking message passing, agents process at their own pace
- **🏢 Enterprise-Grade** -- Built for regulated environments with audit trails and compliance
- **🔄 Interoperable** -- Works alongside A2A, MCP, and existing enterprise systems
