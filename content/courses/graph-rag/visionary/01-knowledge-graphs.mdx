## What Are Knowledge Graphs?

A <JargonTip term="knowledge graph">knowledge graph</JargonTip> represents information as a network of **entities** (nodes) connected by **relationships** (edges). Each fact is stored as a triple: **(Subject, Predicate, Object)**.

  <AnalogyBox emoji="🗺️" title="Think of it like a map of knowledge">A road map shows cities (entities) connected by roads (relationships). A knowledge graph shows concepts connected by their relationships: "Einstein" → developed → "Theory of Relativity".</AnalogyBox>
  <CodeBlock>
{`(Einstein,       developed,    Theory of Relativity)
(Einstein,       born in,      Ulm, Germany)
(Einstein,       won,          Nobel Prize in Physics)
(Nobel Prize,    awarded by,   Royal Swedish Academy)
(Relativity,     foundational to,  Modern Physics)

Following connections: Einstein \u2192 Nobel Prize \u2192 Royal Swedish Academy
  Reveals: The institution that recognized Einstein's work`}
</CodeBlock>
