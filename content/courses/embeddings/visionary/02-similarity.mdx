## Similarity & Distance

Once text is converted to vectors, we can measure how **similar** two pieces of text are by comparing their vectors. The most common method is **<JargonTip term="cosine similarity">cosine similarity</JargonTip>**, which measures the angle between two vectors.

Cosine similarity ranges from -1 (opposite meanings) to 1 (identical meanings). In practice, most text pairs fall between 0 and 1.

| Metric | Range | Best For |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Cosine Similarity | -1 to 1 | Most NLP tasks, semantic search |
| Euclidean Distance | 0 to infinity | Clustering, when magnitude matters |
| Dot Product | -infinity to infinity | Normalized vectors, fast computation |
