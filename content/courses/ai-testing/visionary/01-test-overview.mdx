## AI Testing & Red-Teaming
  <AnalogyBox emoji={'D83DDCA5'} title="Crash Testing for AI">{`AI testing is like crash testing cars \u2014 you intentionally try to break the system in controlled conditions so you can fix vulnerabilities before real users encounter them.`}</AnalogyBox>

A robust AI testing strategy combines <JargonTip term="eval">evaluation</JargonTip> frameworks, <JargonTip term="LLM-as-judge">LLM-as-judge</JargonTip> scoring, <JargonTip term="guardrails">guardrails</JargonTip> testing, and <JargonTip term="drift detection">drift detection</JargonTip> to ensure quality over time.

- **Red-Teaming** -- Adversarial testing: intentionally try to make the AI misbehave
- **Eval Frameworks** -- Systematic measurement of quality, safety, and reliability
- **Injection Defense** -- Testing prompt injection resistance and safety boundaries
- **Regression Testing** -- Ensure updates don't break existing behavior
