## Beyond Naive RAG: Why Loops Beat Pipelines

<JargonTip term="RAG">Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)</JargonTip> in its simplest form is a pipeline: Query → Retrieve → Generate. It works for basic Q&A, but fails catastrophically when retrieval quality is poor — the LLM hallucinates confidently from irrelevant documents.

**Agentic RAG** adds self-correction: the system evaluates its own retrieval quality and retries autonomously. Instead of a pipeline, it's a loop — exactly the pattern you learned in Week 1 with LangGraph.

<AnalogyBox emoji="📚" title="Library Research vs Google Search">
Naive RAG is like Googling a question and reading the first result. Agentic RAG is like a research librarian: they search, evaluate whether the sources actually answer your question, try different search terms if results are poor, cross-reference multiple sources, and only hand you a synthesis when they're confident in the quality. The librarian loops. Google doesn't.
</AnalogyBox>

### The Five-Stage Agentic RAG Pipeline

  ['2. Retrieve','Single query, top-k results','Query → retrieve → evaluate → rewrite → retry loop'],
  ['3. Grade','None — trust whatever comes back','LLM-as-judge scores relevance of each document'],
  ['4. Generate','Single LLM call on retrieved docs','Generate + hallucination check against sources'],
  ['5. Validate','None','Check answer is grounded in retrieved docs, retry if not'],
]}/>

<AnalogyBox emoji="🏭" title="MDM Data Quality Pipeline">
If you've built data quality workflows at McKesson, Agentic RAG will feel familiar. It's the same pattern: ingest data → validate against rules → if validation fails, route to enrichment/correction → re-validate → only promote to golden record when quality threshold is met. The "promote to golden record" moment is when the RAG system finally generates its answer.
</AnalogyBox>

### When to Use Agentic RAG vs Naive RAG

Not every use case needs self-correction. Use the simpler approach when it's sufficient:

- **Naive RAG**: Internal FAQ bots, simple document Q&A, low-stakes queries where "close enough" is fine
- **Agentic RAG**: Contract analysis, regulatory compliance, medical information, supplier onboarding — anywhere wrong answers have real consequences
