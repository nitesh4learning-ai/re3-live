## When to Fine-Tune

<JargonTip term="fine-tuning">Fine-tuning</JargonTip> is powerful but expensive. Before committing to it, you should exhaust cheaper alternatives: **prompt engineering**, **few-shot examples**, and **RAG**. Fine-tuning wins when you need consistent style, domain-specific behavior, or reduced <JargonTip term="inference">inference</JargonTip> latency from a smaller model.

  <AnalogyBox emoji="🎨" title="Think of it like training an artist">Prompt engineering is giving an artist detailed instructions for each painting. Fine-tuning is teaching the artist your preferred style so they naturally paint that way every time -- no lengthy instructions needed.</AnalogyBox>
  <ComparisonTable title="Approaches Compared" columns={['Approach','Cost','Latency','When It Wins']} rows={[
    ['Prompt Engineering','Low (per-token only)','Base model latency','Simple formatting, general tasks'],
    ['Few-Shot Prompting','Medium (more tokens)','Higher (longer context)','Pattern matching with examples'],
    ['RAG','Medium (retrieval infra)','Higher (retrieval + gen)','Domain knowledge from documents'],
    ['Fine-Tuning','High (training + hosting)','Lower (smaller model)','Consistent style, specialized behavior'],
  ]}/>
  <DecisionTreeExplorer/>
  <ExpandableSection title="Cost-Benefit Analysis" icon="💰">

Fine-tuning costs include: **data preparation** (10-50 hours), **training compute** ($5-500+ depending on model size), **evaluation** (ongoing), and **hosting** (dedicated endpoint). The payoff comes from: reduced prompt tokens (no few-shot examples needed), lower <JargonTip term="inference">inference</JargonTip> latency, and more consistent outputs.

    <CodeBlock>
{`# Cost comparison: prompting vs fine-tuning
prompt_tokens_per_call = 2000  # With few-shot examples
ft_tokens_per_call = 200       # No examples needed

calls_per_day = 10000
cost_per_1k_tokens = 0.003

# Prompting: $60/day
prompt_cost = (prompt_tokens_per_call / 1000) * cost_per_1k_tokens * calls_per_day

# Fine-tuned: $6/day + $200 training (one-time)
ft_cost = (ft_tokens_per_call / 1000) * cost_per_1k_tokens * calls_per_day
training_cost = 200  # one-time

# Break-even in ~4 days at scale
breakeven_days = training_cost / (prompt_cost - ft_cost)`}
</CodeBlock>
