## The Problem RAG Solves

LLMs have a fundamental limitation: they can only use knowledge from their training data. They don't know about events after their training cutoff, your company's internal documents, or real-time data. When they lack knowledge, they <JargonTip term="hallucination">hallucinate</JargonTip> -- generating plausible-sounding but incorrect information.

<JargonTip term="RAG">**Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)**</JargonTip> solves this by fetching relevant information from external sources and injecting it into the prompt before the LLM generates a response.

<AnalogyBox emoji="📚" title="Think of it like an open-book exam">An LLM without RAG is a student taking an exam from memory -- they might mix up facts. An LLM with RAG is a student with the textbook open -- they can look up the right answers.</AnalogyBox>
